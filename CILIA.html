AOBS

AOBS (Acousto Optical Beam Splitter) uses a crystal instead of fixed wavelength mirrors to split the excitation from emission light. This way, AOBS can better separate close colors like GFP from YFP. We have this AOBS technology only on our Leica SP5 confocal microscope!

PDF info


Spectral detectors

In a spectral detector system, light is divided into multiple spectral bands. The bands are independent and continuously tunable within the full visible spectrum. The light in each band is detected by a light sensor (3 in Leica, 2 in our Olympus confocal). This setup allows fluorescence emission ranges to be freely adjustable.

DSU

The DSU (Disk Scanning Unit) disk contains a pattern of slits that creates a virtual pinhole as the disk spins at 3,000 to 5,600 rpm. It's designed to optimize the trade-off between confocality and light throughput. However, we have now a better option, the spinning disk confocal microscope.

Fluorescence

Fluorescence is a process in which matter absorbs light and re-emits at a different wavelength. All our microscopes in the lab uses fluorescence. Be sure to check out this video that describes the principles of fluorescence and fluorescence microscopy!


Light sources and filters

CI Lab has several microscopes with different light sources and fluorescence filters. Knowing which microscope has what kind of light source and filters can be very helpful to find the best microscope for your experiments.

Laser Capture Microdissection


Laser Capture Microdissection (LCM) enables you to collect areas from single cells up to areas of several thousand square-micrometer for further DNA, RNA and protein analysis. Even the catapulting of live cells with later cultivation is possible!

Animal imaging

Live animal brain imaging with 2 photon microscope allows in vivo deep tissue imaging. This microscope is mainly used by Dr. Krizbai Istvan's group for neuroscience research. The microscope is manufactured by a Hungarian company. Here is an interesting video (in hungarian) about the company.
